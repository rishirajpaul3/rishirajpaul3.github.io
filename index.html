The long way round
to building systems.

I started on the ops floor — managing 50+ sales reps across East India, calling every broken handoff, every missed follow-up, every CRM that nobody used correctly. I knew the process better than anyone. I just couldn't fix the tools.

So I got an MSc in Entrepreneurship from Queen Mary University of London, came to the UK, and taught myself to code — not to become a developer, but because I was tired of waiting for someone else to fix the infrastructure around sales.

Now I build AI-powered GTM systems from scratch — outbound infrastructure, lead enrichment pipelines, CRM automation, and sales co-pilots that run in production. I've been on both sides: the rep who needed the tool, and the engineer who built it.

That's the difference. Most GTM engineers know the code. I know the job the code is supposed to do.
